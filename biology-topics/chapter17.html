Chapter 17 - Biotechnology and Its Applications

1. Principles of Biotechnology - Short Notes

Genetic Engineering (Recombinant DNA Technology)

  • Involves altering the genetic material of an organism to achieve desired traits.
  • Steps: Isolation of DNA, cutting with restriction enzymes, gene cloning, and expression in host cells.
  • Example: E. coli producing human insulin.

Cell and Tissue Culture

  • Growing cells or tissues in controlled lab conditions (in vitro).
  • Used in research, pharmaceutical production, and plant breeding.
  • Example: Plant cloning and mammalian cell culture for protein production.

PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction)

  • Technique to amplify a specific DNA segment.
  • Steps: Denaturation, annealing, and extension.
  • Used in diagnostics, forensics, and genetic research.

Cloning

  • Creating genetically identical organisms or cells.
  • Gene cloning for producing proteins; organism cloning for research.
  • Example: Dolly the sheep (first cloned mammal).

Recombinant Protein Production

  • Using organisms (bacteria, yeast, mammalian cells) to produce proteins like insulin, hormones, and enzymes.
  • Example: Insulin production in E. coli.

2. Applications of Biotechnology - Short Notes

i. Medicine

Gene Therapy

  • Involves inserting, altering, or removing genes within an individualâ€™s cells to treat genetic disorders.
  • Example: Treatment of genetic diseases like cystic fibrosis.

Vaccine Production

  • Biotechnology allows the production of vaccines using recombinant DNA technology.
  • Example: Hepatitis B vaccine produced using yeast cells.

Monoclonal Antibodies

  • Used for diagnostic and therapeutic purposes by targeting specific cells (e.g., cancer cells).
  • Example: Trastuzumab (Herceptin) for breast cancer.

Biopharmaceuticals

  • Production of therapeutic proteins such as insulin, growth hormones, and clotting factors.
  • Example: Insulin production using E. coli.

ii. Agriculture

Genetically Modified Organisms (GMOs)

  • Altering the genetic material of crops for better traits like pest resistance, higher yield, and drought tolerance.
  • Example: Bt cotton, Golden Rice (enhanced with Vitamin A).

Microbial Fertilizers and Bio-pesticides

  • Microorganisms are used to improve soil health, enhance crop growth, and protect plants from pests.
  • Example: Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) as a natural pesticide.

Gene Editing

  • Using technologies like CRISPR to modify plant genes for improved traits.
  • Example: Development of disease-resistant crops.

iii. Environmental Biotechnology

Bioremediation

  • The use of microorganisms to break down pollutants and clean up the environment.
  • Example: Using Pseudomonas bacteria to clean up oil spills.

Waste Treatment

  • Microorganisms degrade organic matter in sewage treatment plants to purify water.
  • Example: Activated sludge process in wastewater treatment.

Biofuels

  • Using microorganisms to produce ethanol, methane, and biodiesel from renewable resources.
  • Example: Production of bioethanol from corn or sugarcane.

iv. Industrial Biotechnology

Fermentation

  • Microbial fermentation is used to produce food, beverages, and biofuels.
  • Example: Brewing of beer, yogurt production, and ethanol production.

Enzyme Production

  • Industrial production of enzymes used in food processing, textiles, detergents, and pharmaceuticals.
  • Example: Amylases used in baking and detergents.

Bioplastics

  • Biodegradable plastics produced using microorganisms, reducing environmental pollution.
  • Example: PHA (Polyhydroxyalkanoates) produced by bacteria.
