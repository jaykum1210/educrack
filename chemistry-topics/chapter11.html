Chapter 11 - p-Block Elements

1. Trends in Groups 13-18 (p-Block Elements)

General Trends

Trend Group 13 (Boron) Group 14 (Carbon) Group 15 (Nitrogen) Group 16 (Oxygen) Group 17 (Halogens) Group 18 (Noble Gases)

Group 13: The Boron Family

  • Elements: Boron (B), Aluminum (Al), Gallium (Ga), Indium (In), Thallium (Tl)
  • General Configuration: ns² np¹
  • Trends:
    • Metallic Character: Increases down (B = metalloid, others = metals).
    • Oxidation States: +3 (Stable), +1 (Tl prefers +1 due to the inert pair effect).

Group 14: The Carbon Family

  • Elements: Carbon (C), Silicon (Si), Germanium (Ge), Tin (Sn), Lead (Pb)
  • General Configuration: ns² np²
  • Trends:
    • Covalency Dominates: Carbon forms strong covalent bonds.
    • Metallic Character: Increases down.
    • Oxidation States: +4 (C, Si, Ge), +2 (Pb & Sn).

Group 15: The Nitrogen Family

  • Elements: Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), Arsenic (As), Antimony (Sb), Bismuth (Bi)
  • General Configuration: ns² np³
  • Trends:
    • Oxidation States: -3 (N & P), +3, +5 (As, Sb, Bi).
    • Metallic Character: Increases down.

Group 16: The Oxygen Family

  • Elements: Oxygen (O), Sulfur (S), Selenium (Se), Tellurium (Te), Polonium (Po)
  • General Configuration: ns² np⁴
  • Trends:
    • Oxidation States: -2 (common), +4, +6 (S, Se, Te).
    • Metallic Character: Increases down.

Group 17: The Halogens

  • Elements: Fluorine (F), Chlorine (Cl), Bromine (Br), Iodine (I), Astatine (At)
  • General Configuration: ns² np⁵
  • Trends:
    • Oxidation State: -1 (most stable), +1, +3, +5, +7 (Cl, Br, I).
    • Reactivity: Decreases down the group (F > Cl > Br > I > At).

Group 18: The Noble Gases

  • Elements: Helium (He), Neon (Ne), Argon (Ar), Krypton (Kr), Xenon (Xe), Radon (Rn)
  • General Configuration: ns² np⁶
  • Trends:
    • Oxidation State: Mostly 0 (Xe can form +2, +4, +6 compounds).
    • Reactivity: Almost inert.

2. Key Compounds & Their Properties

i. Group 1 & 2: Alkali & Alkaline Earth Metal Compounds

Compound Formula Properties & Uses
Sodium Hydroxide (Caustic Soda) NaOH Strong base, used in soaps, paper-making
Sodium Carbonate (Washing Soda) Na₂CO₃ Used in glass-making, water softening
Sodium Bicarbonate (Baking Soda) NaHCO₃ Mild base, used in cooking, medicine
Calcium Carbonate (Limestone, Chalk, Marble) CaCO₃ Found in nature, used in cement, shells
Calcium Hydroxide (Slaked Lime) Ca(OH)₂ Used in mortar, neutralizing acids
Calcium Sulfate (Gypsum, Plaster of Paris) CaSO₄ · 2H₂O / CaSO₄ · ½H₂O Used in construction, medical bandages
Magnesium Sulfate (Epsom Salt) MgSO₄ Used as a laxative, in fertilizers

ii. Group 13: Boron & Aluminum Compounds

  • Boric Acid (H₃BO₃): Mild antiseptic, used in eye drops
  • Borax (Na₂B₄O₇ · 10H₂O): Used in detergents, glass-making
  • Aluminum Oxide (Al₂O₃): Hard, used in abrasives, ceramics
  • Aluminum Sulfate (Al₂(SO₄)₃): Used in water purification

iii. Group 14: Carbon & Silicon Compounds

  • Carbon Dioxide (CO₂): Greenhouse gas, used in soft drinks
  • Carbon Monoxide (CO): Toxic gas, used in metallurgy
  • Silicon Dioxide (SiO₂): Found in glass, semiconductors
  • Silicones: Heat-resistant, used in lubricants

iv. Group 15: Nitrogen & Phosphorus Compounds

  • Ammonia (NH₃): Basic gas, used in fertilizers
  • Nitric Acid (HNO₃): Strong acid, used in explosives (TNT)
  • Phosphoric Acid (H₃PO₄): Used in soft drinks, fertilizers
  • Red & White Phosphorus (P): White P is highly reactive, Red P is stable

v. Group 16: Oxygen & Sulfur Compounds

  • Water (H₂O): Essential for life
  • Hydrogen Peroxide (H₂O₂): Used as a disinfectant, bleach
  • Sulfuric Acid (H₂SO₄): Strong acid, used in batteries, industry
  • Sulfur Dioxide (SO₂): Pollutant, used in preservatives

vi. Group 17: Halogen Compounds

  • Hydrochloric Acid (HCl): Strong acid, used in digestion (stomach acid)
  • Sodium Chloride (NaCl): Essential for life, used in food
  • Silver Bromide (AgBr): Light-sensitive, used in photography
  • Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs): Used in refrigeration (damaging to ozone layer)

vii. Group 18: Noble Gas Compounds

  • Xenon Fluoride (XeF₂, XeF₄): Used in lasers, research
  • Neon Gas (Ne): Used in lighting

viii. Miscellaneous Important Compounds

  • Potassium Permanganate (KMnO₄): Strong oxidizer, used in disinfectants
  • Calcium Hypochlorite (Bleaching Powder) (Ca(OCl)₂): Used for water purification
  • Zinc Sulfate (ZnSO₄): Used in medicine, supplements
