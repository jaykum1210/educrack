Chapter 10 - s-Block Elements

1. Alkali Metals (Group 1 Elements)

Introduction

Alkali metals are highly reactive metals found in Group 1 of the periodic table.

  • Elements in Group 1:
    • Lithium (Li)
    • Sodium (Na)
    • Potassium (K)
    • Rubidium (Rb)
    • Cesium (Cs)
    • Francium (Fr) (Radioactive)
  • General Electronic Configuration: [Noble Gas]ns1 (One valence electron in the outer shell)

Physical Properties

Property Trend in Group 1
Softness Increases down the group
Melting & Boiling Points Decrease down the group
Density Increases down the group (except K)
Atomic Radius Increases down the group
Ionization Energy Decreases down the group (easier to lose an electron)
Conductivity Good conductors of heat & electricity

Why are they soft? They have a weak metallic bond due to only one valence electron.

Chemical Properties

Highly Reactive: Easily lose one electron, forming +1 ions (M⁺). Reactivity increases down the group (Cs > Rb > K > Na > Li).

(a) Reaction with Water:

2M + 2H2O → 2M+ + 2OH- + H2↑

  • Lithium (Li): Gentle reaction, bubbles of H₂ gas.
  • Sodium (Na): Fizzes rapidly, moves on water surface.
  • Potassium (K): Ignites, burns with a purple flame.
  • Rubidium (Rb) & Cesium (Cs): Explosively react with water!

(b) Reaction with Oxygen (Forms Oxides & Peroxides)

4M + O2 → 2M2O

Types of Oxides:

  • Lithium (Li₂O) → Normal Oxide
  • Sodium (Na₂O₂) → Peroxide
  • K, Rb, Cs → Superoxides (MO₂)

(c) Reaction with Halogens (Forms Halides, MX)

2M + X2 → 2MX (X = F, Cl, Br, I)

Example: Na + Cl₂ → NaCl

Alkali metal halides are ionic, soluble in water, and have high melting points.

Biological Importance

  • Sodium (Na⁺): Regulates nerve function & blood pressure.
  • Potassium (K⁺): Essential for muscle contractions.
  • Lithium (Li): Used in treating bipolar disorder.

Uses of Alkali Metals

  • Lithium: Used in batteries, antidepressant drugs.
  • Sodium: Used in table salt (NaCl), streetlights (Na vapor lamps).
  • Potassium: Used in fertilizers (KCl, K₂SO₄).
  • Cesium & Rubidium: Used in atomic clocks & electronics.

2. Alkaline Earth Metals (Group 2 Elements)

Introduction

  • Alkaline Earth Metals are highly reactive metals found in Group 2 of the periodic table.
  • Elements in Group 2:
    • Beryllium (Be)
    • Magnesium (Mg)
    • Calcium (Ca)
    • Strontium (Sr)
    • Barium (Ba)
    • Radium (Ra) (Radioactive)
  • General Electronic Configuration: [Noble Gas]ns2

Physical Properties

Property Trend in Group 2
Hardness Harder than Group 1 metals
Melting & Boiling Points Higher than alkali metals but decrease down the group
Density Increases down the group
Atomic Radius Increases down the group
Ionization Energy Decreases down the group (easier to lose electrons)
Conductivity Good conductors of heat & electricity

Chemical Properties

  • Reactive but less than Alkali Metals:
    • Easily lose two electrons, forming +2 ions (M²⁺).
    • Reactivity increases down the group (Ba > Sr > Ca > Mg > Be).
  • Reaction with Water:

    • M + 2H2O → M(OH)2 + H2↑ (M = Mg, Ca, Sr, Ba)

    • Be & Mg: React very slowly with cold water.
    • Ca, Sr, Ba: React faster, forming hydroxides & releasing H₂ gas.
    • Ba: Very reactive with water!
  • Reaction with Oxygen:

    • 2M + O2 → 2MO

    Example: Ca + O2 → CaO (Quicklime)

  • Reaction with Halogens:

    • M + X2 → MX2 (X = F, Cl, Br, I)

    Example: Mg + Cl2 → MgCl2

  • Reaction with Acids:

    • M + 2HCl → MCl2 + H2↑

    Example: Mg + 2HCl → MgCl2 + H2

Biological Importance

  • Calcium (Ca²⁺): Essential for bones, teeth, muscle contractions.
  • Magnesium (Mg²⁺): Important for enzymes, chlorophyll in plants.
  • Beryllium (Be): Toxic to humans, not biologically important.

Uses of Alkaline Earth Metals

  • Beryllium: Used in aerospace, X-ray windows.
  • Magnesium: Used in fireworks, alloys, medicines (Milk of Magnesia).
  • Calcium: Used in cement (CaO), bones & teeth, water treatment.
  • Strontium & Barium: Used in fireworks, medical imaging (BaSO₄ for X-rays).
  • Radium: Used in radiotherapy (cancer treatment) but is highly radioactive.
